About

Controllable Biology is an academic initiative focused on modeling, identifying, and controlling biological systems with rigor and practical relevance.

Motivation

Biological systems exhibit rich, multi-scale dynamics. Making interventions trustworthy requires methods that integrate predictive modeling with causal and mechanistic structure.

Approach

We emphasize transparent assumptions, robust evaluation, and an end-to-end view of intervention pipelines—from data and models to policies and experimental feedback.

Community

The initiative supports workshops, curated talks, and collaboration across machine learning, statistics, control theory, and the life sciences.

Themes

Representative directions that connect learning and decision-making for biological intervention.

  • Causal discovery and identifiability for biological networks and dynamical systems
  • Control and reinforcement learning with safety, uncertainty, and constraints
  • Mechanistic and hybrid modeling combining structure with scalable ML
  • Experimental design and adaptive interventions with feedback loops
  • Evaluation: counterfactual validation, robustness, and generalization
